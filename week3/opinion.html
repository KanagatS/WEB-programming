This is my 3rd time going through the Wild Hunt, but for some reason now it seems to me that I went through it for the last time. After reading the entire book saga and going through the first 2 parts, the impressions of the finale of the story about the White Wolf are completely different. A lot of emotions intertwined into one: some characters are happy, others are sad, you sit with a bewildered happy smile on your face, saying: “Is that it? Is there really nothing else? No, it can not be". But in the end, when you return to places where you have already been, with which your memories are connected, where emotions are seething, and you don’t see ... anyone and nothing, just emptiness - at such moments, such a wave of longing will come over you that you want corny to load the last save and again be close to those characters who were once here. It was damn sad for me to return to Kaer Morhen after the adventures of the game, to see that it was empty and there was not a single soul in it, I remembered the last conversation between Geralt and Eskel and realized that the witchers of the School of the Wolf would never return to their home ... from -due to the death of my mentor, my father, I realized that they have gone to different parts of the world and may never meet again ... we will never meet to know about it.
